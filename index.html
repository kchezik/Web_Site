About Me

I'm a PhD candidate at Simon Fraser University in Burnaby, British Columbia. Originally hailing from the St. Croix River valley in east-central Minnesota, I grew up at the nexus of glaciated prairie potholes, driftless deciduous forest and morained coniferous iron-range. These varied landscapes have long inspired me and fueled my interest in ecology, geology and all the sciences upon which the natural world is built.

Although my interests have long centered on the environment I've also become keenly interested in the field of computer science and statistics with specific interest in using statistical tools, learning algorithms and spatial analysis (GIS) to understand ecosystem complexity and function at large scales. Finally, I'm also interested in the field of data visualization or sometimes referred to as data journalism. I find I'm a strong visual learner and can engage with data at multiple levels when displayed beautifully and succinctly. With all my work I try to make it interactive, engaging and digestible so that all might see the forest as well as the trees.
